# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Last Updated:** 2025-01

---

## Primary Persona: Executive Decision-Maker

**Title:** C-suite, VP, Director, Head of Strategy/Innovation  
**Company Size:** 50-5000 employees (mid-market to enterprise)  
**Industry:** Professional services, SaaS, financial services, healthcare, consulting

**Characteristics:**
- **Non-technical:** Understands business strategy but not software architecture
- **Time-constrained:** Needs clarity fast, won't engage with complex tools
- **ROI-focused:** Must justify AI investments to board or stakeholders
- **Skeptical but curious:** Recognizes AI hype but sees real potential

**Pain Points:**
- "I know we need AI, but I don't know where to start"
- "My team wastes time debating vague AI ideas"
- "Consultants give me 80-page decks I can't implement"
- "We tried an AI pilot but it went nowhere"

**Goals:**
- De-risk AI initiatives with clear roadmaps
- Align internal stakeholders on priorities
- Build credibility with board/investors on AI strategy
- Accelerate time-to-value for AI projects

---

## Secondary Persona: Innovation Lead

**Title:** Head of Digital Transformation, Innovation Manager, Strategy Consultant  
**Company Size:** 100-10,000 employees  
**Industry:** Consulting firms, technology companies, growth-stage startups

**Characteristics:**
- **Bridge role:** Between business and technical teams
- **Implementation-focused:** Needs concrete next steps, not theory
- **Resource-constrained:** Limited budget for external consultants
- **Accountable for outcomes:** Measured on successful AI adoption

**Pain Points:**
- "Executives ask for AI but don't know what they want"
- "Technical teams roll their eyes at vague AI requests"
- "We need to upskill teams but training is expensive"
- "Pilots succeed but don't scale"

**Goals:**
- Translate business needs into technical requirements
- Build internal AI capabilities without large budgets
- Demonstrate measurable impact from AI initiatives
- Create repeatable frameworks for AI adoption

---

## Tertiary Persona: B2B Partner (Consulting Firm/VC)

**Profile:** Management consultancy, venture capital firm, accelerator program  
**Size:** 10-500 employees  
**Focus:** Advising portfolio companies or clients on AI strategy

**Characteristics:**
- **Client-facing:** Needs tools to deliver value faster
- **Scalability-focused:** Wants repeatable processes for multiple clients
- **Brand-conscious:** Outputs must reflect their expertise
- **ROI-driven:** Tools must improve margins or client outcomes

**Pain Points:**
- "Creating custom AI strategies for every client is time-intensive"
- "Clients need ongoing support we can't afford to provide"
- "Our team lacks deep AI expertise across all industries"
- "Deliverables take too long and clients lose momentum"

**Goals:**
- Accelerate strategy delivery for portfolio companies
- Standardize AI advisory frameworks
- Enable junior consultants to deliver senior-level outputs
- Improve client retention through ongoing value

---

## Anti-Personas (Who We Don't Serve)

**1. Deep Tech Startups**
- Already have technical expertise
- Need custom ML/AI model development, not business strategy

**2. Individual Hobbyists**
- Not facing organizational challenges
- Can't afford B2B pricing

**3. Large Enterprises with Dedicated AI Teams**
- Already have internal resources and frameworks
- Need specialized consulting, not self-service tools

**4. Solo Entrepreneurs without Teams**
- Literacy modules assume team-based learning
- Blueprints are overkill for single-person operations

## Market Segmentation

### By Company Size:
- **50-200 employees:** High-growth startups, need fast execution
- **200-1000 employees:** Mid-market, struggle with alignment and scale
- **1000-5000 employees:** Enterprise, complex org structures, slow adoption

### By Industry:
- **Professional Services:** (law, accounting, consulting) - high margin, client-facing
- **SaaS/Tech:** Early AI adopters, technical but need business alignment
- **Financial Services:** Regulated, risk-averse, need compliance-ready plans
- **Healthcare:** Data-rich, complex workflows, strict privacy requirements

### By Use Case:
- **Operational Efficiency:** Automate workflows, reduce costs
- **Customer Experience:** Chatbots, personalization, support automation
- **Data Intelligence:** Analytics, forecasting, decision support
- **Product Innovation:** AI-powered features, new offerings

---

## Geographic Focus

**Primary:** North America (US, Canada)  
**Secondary:** UK, Western Europe, Australia  
**Language:** English-first (expandable to other languages)

---

## Buying Behavior

**Budget Authority:**
- Executives: $10K-$100K+ annual software budgets
- Innovation Leads: $5K-$50K discretionary spend
- B2B Partners: Volume-based, per-seat or per-client pricing

**Decision Timeline:**
- Evaluation: 1-2 weeks (fast decision cycle)
- Pilot: 30-90 days (proof of value)
- Full adoption: 3-12 months (org-wide rollout)

**Procurement:**
- Credit card purchase for <$10K
- Procurement/Legal review for >$10K
- Multi-year contracts for enterprise

---

## ICP Scoring Criteria

**High-fit indicators:**
- Non-technical executive with AI mandate
- 100-1000 employee company
- Professional services or SaaS industry
- Active AI pilot or exploration phase
- Budget authority >$10K

**Medium-fit indicators:**
- Innovation/Strategy role (not C-suite)
- 50-100 or 1000-5000 employees
- Adjacent industries (retail, manufacturing)
- Exploring AI but no active projects

**Low-fit indicators:**
- Technical role (engineer, data scientist)
- <50 or >5000 employees
- Deep tech or highly technical industry
- No budget authority
